Vinyasa Yoga

Vinyasa Yoga is a dynamic yoga style where breathing and movement are synchronized. In addition to practicing similarities to Hatha Yoga, the roots of this style of yoga are also linked to the Ashtanga Yoga system. Although the series structure varies and differs from the traditional Ashtanga series. Through ever-changing series, this yoga practice experiences the benefits and necessities of change and learns to love it.

Ardha Matsyendrasana (half spinal twist)

You can work on a sitting twist with this position. The initial position is sitting on the floor with your legs stretched out. Then bend one knee and place your foot on the other side of the stretched leg, place your arm around the outer side of your knee, twist your back towards the opposite side and rest the other hand on the floor as you can see in the picture.

Viparita Dandasana

Dwi pada viparita dandasana or Two-Legged Inverted Staff Pose is an advanced inverted asana that requires a great deal of flexibility, especially in the back. It is a combination of urdhva dhanurasana (wheel pose) and sirsasana (headstand).

Ashtanga Yoga

Ashtanga is a yoga system introduced to the modern world by Sri K. Pattabhi Jois in India. The peculiarity of Ashtanga vinyasa yoga is the combination of different postures or asanas with the vinyasa system. Vinyasa means synchronizing breathing and movement.
